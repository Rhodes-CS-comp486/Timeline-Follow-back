Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as Slot Machines?

Any game where you insert money (or credits) and spin reels for a randomized outcome. This includes traditional slot machines at a casino, video slots, electronic gaming machines (EGMs), and video poker terminals.

Examples: penny slots, progressive jackpot slots, video poker machines

What counts as Table Games?

Casino games played at a table against the house or other players, typically managed by a dealer.

Examples: blackjack, poker, roulette, baccarat, craps, pai gow

What counts as Sports/Event Betting?

Wagering money on the outcome of a sporting event, competition, or other real-world event, whether placed in person or through an app/website.

Examples: football parlays, point spreads, moneylines, horse racing, esports betting, political election betting

What counts as Lottery/Draw Games?

Games where you purchase a ticket or entry and a winner is chosen by a random draw.

Examples: Powerball, Mega Millions, scratch-off tickets, keno, daily number draws, raffle tickets

What counts as Online Gambling?

Any gambling activity conducted over the internet that does not fit another category above — typically through a dedicated gambling website or app.

Examples: online casino slots, virtual table games on a gambling site, online bingo, daily fantasy sports (DFS) contests

How do I calculate how much I won or lost?

Enter the net total for the entire session — meaning your overall result after all wins and losses are combined. If you won some bets but lost more than you won overall, the number will be negative.

Formula: money you left with − money you started with = net win/loss. Use a positive number if you came out ahead and a negative number if you came out behind.

Example: You started with $100, won $80 on one bet, then lost $150 on later bets and left with $30. Your net is $30 − $100 = −$70.

Example: You started with $50 and left with $120. Your net is $120 − $50 = +$70.
